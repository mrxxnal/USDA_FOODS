Introduction

"The USDA Food Analysis Project aims to analyze nutritional content, categories, and trends across various food items, leveraging data science techniques. Poor nutritional habits contribute to several public health challenges, including obesity, diabetes, and cardiovascular diseases. Understanding food composition and consumption patterns is critical to addressing these issues. In recent years, the USDA FoodData Central repository has emerged as a valuable resource for detailed food-related data, offering an opportunity to explore relationships between different foods and their health impacts.

The USDA dataset covers a wide range of food items, from fresh produce to highly processed snacks, capturing information on calories, protein, carbohydrates, and fats, among other nutrients. Our project aims to identify trends and patterns in these nutritional values, especially in ultra-processed food categories. Ultra-processed foods have been linked to higher rates of non-communicable diseases due to their high sugar, fat, and sodium content. By analyzing these foods, this project seeks to provide actionable insights that promote healthier eating habits and policymaking.

In this project, advanced data science methods such as Principal Component Analysis (PCA), Clustering, and Support Vector Machines (SVM) are applied to identify key patterns and make predictions about food categories. Additionally, this study explores the potential for leveraging machine learning to create more personalized dietary recommendations based on food composition data.

Beyond its technical goals, the project underscores the importance of data-driven decision-making in nutrition and public health. By visualizing trends and communicating key findings effectively, this work aims to bridge the gap between technical analysis and real-world applications, inspiring healthier dietary choices and more informed public policies.

The USDA Food Analysis Project is an effort to empower individuals and policymakers with data insights that can foster a healthier future. Join us on this journey as we delve into data, uncover trends, and propose actionable conclusions for better nutrition and public health.
