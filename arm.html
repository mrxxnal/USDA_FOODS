Introduction to Association Rule Mining (ARM)

Association Rule Mining (ARM) is a powerful machine learning technique used to uncover hidden patterns and relationships within large datasets. It is widely applied in **market basket analysis, fraud detection, recommendation systems, and medical diagnostics.** The goal is to identify items that frequently occur together and derive actionable insights.

Key Concepts in ARM

ARM is based on three fundamental concepts:

  • Support: Measures how frequently an itemset appears in the dataset.
  • Confidence: Evaluates how often item B is purchased when item A is purchased.
  • Lift: Measures how much more likely items are bought together compared to random chance.

Support

Support measures how often a set of items appears in transactions. A high support value indicates a frequent pattern.

Formula:
Support(A) = (Number of transactions containing A) / (Total transactions)

Confidence

Confidence tells us how likely item B is to be purchased when item A is purchased.

Formula:
Confidence(A → B) = Support(A ∪ B) / Support(A)

Lift

Lift measures the strength of an association rule relative to the expected frequency if A and B were independent.

Formula:
Lift(A → B) = Support(A ∪ B) / (Support(A) × Support(B))

Association Rules

Association rules help identify relationships between items in a dataset. A rule is written as:

A → B (If A is purchased, then B is likely to be purchased)

**Example:** If a customer buys **milk and bread**, they are **likely to buy butter**.
Rule: **{Milk, Bread} → {Butter}**

The Apriori Algorithm

The **Apriori Algorithm** is a widely used method for mining association rules. It identifies frequent itemsets and derives rules based on support, confidence, and lift values.

How Apriori Works

1. Scan the dataset and calculate **support** for individual items. 2. Remove items that do not meet the **minimum support threshold**. 3. Generate **larger itemsets** iteratively and filter based on support. 4. Generate **association rules** from the frequent itemsets.

Why Use Apriori?

- Efficiently finds frequent itemsets in large datasets. - Helps businesses optimize product placement and marketing strategies. - Useful in **fraud detection, medical diagnosis, and bioinformatics.**
